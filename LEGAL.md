# Legal information

This document is not legal advice. It summarizes how PES3-Disc is intended to be used and published.

## What PES3-Disc is

PES3-Disc is a **helper tool** that detects PlayStation 3–format discs on your PC, optionally decrypts **retail** discs using the PS3 Disc Dumper engine, stages game data to a local folder, and launches **your** RPCS3 installation.

PES3-Disc does **not** include RPCS3, PlayStation system software, games, decryption keys, or circumvention devices.

## Your responsibilities

- You must **own** any physical disc you decrypt or copy.
- You must comply with **copyright and anti-circumvention laws** in your country. In many regions, decrypting or copying disc content you do not own is unlawful.
- You must comply with the **RPCS3** license and terms when using RPCS3.
- IRD keys and similar data are fetched by third-party libraries; use them only for discs you own.
- You must **not redistribute** decrypted or copied game files (upload, torrent, sell, share folders, etc.).

## Prohibited uses

Do not use PES3-Disc to:

- Process discs, images, or files you do not own or lack permission to use.
- Enable piracy, mass copying, or public distribution of game content.
- Operate a commercial service based on decrypted or copied material.
- Circumvent access controls for unlicensed content.

The software may include in-app acknowledgments before decrypt or copy operations; accepting them records your agreement to these responsibilities for the stated terms version. That flow is for **clarity**, not a waiver of law.

## Persistent cache

If you disable “delete cache when RPCS3 exits,” decrypted or staged files remain on your storage device until you delete them. That is intended for **personal, local convenience** (faster replay). It does not:

- Transfer or license copyrights.
- Make sharing decrypted files lawful.
- Change anti-circumvention obligations in your jurisdiction.

You are responsible for securing and deleting local copies as you see fit.

## Data on your computer

Decrypt output and cache folders contain game data derived from your disc. They stay on your machine unless **you** copy them elsewhere. Optional bug reports are described in [PRIVACY.md](PRIVACY.md); they do not include disc contents.
