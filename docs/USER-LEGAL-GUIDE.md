# User guide — staying within the rules

This is plain-language guidance, **not legal advice**. Laws differ by country; when in doubt, ask a qualified lawyer.

## What PES3-Disc does on your PC

- Reads **your** optical disc or mounted volume.
- May **decrypt** retail PS3 discs (using the PS3 Disc Dumper engine) into a folder on **your** disk.
- May **copy** DIY / burned layouts into a cache folder.
- Launches **your** RPCS3 install — it does not include games, keys, or RPCS3 itself.

## What you should do

| Do | Why |
|----|-----|
| Use only discs **you own** | Copyright and circumvention rules generally assume lawful access to the media you bought. |
| Keep decrypted files **private** on your machine | Treat them like a personal backup, not something to share. |
| Use **persistent cache** if you want convenience | Faster replay; same ownership rules apply. |
| Read [LEGAL.md](../LEGAL.md) | Full project terms and disclaimers. |

## What you must not do

- Decrypt or copy discs you **do not own** (rental, borrowed, downloaded ISO of a game you never bought, etc.).
- **Upload**, torrent, sell, or give decrypted folders to others.
- Use PES3-Disc in a **commercial** service (e.g. “play any disc” kiosk).
- Assume the app **grants** any right from Sony, publishers, or RPCS3.

## Persistent cache

Keeping decrypt output on an SSD is a **convenience** feature. It does not change who owns the copyright or whether copying/decryption is allowed where you live. You are still responsible for the files on disk.

## Bug reports

Optional bug reports send only title, description, and system metadata — **not** disc contents. See [PRIVACY.md](../PRIVACY.md).

## If you redistribute PES3-Disc builds

Include `LICENSE`, `LEGAL.md`, and `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md` with the installer or ZIP. Do not bundle games, IRD keys, or decrypted content.
